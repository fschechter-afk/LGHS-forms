## Mission Statement

Lubavitch Girls High School is committed to Kindling the Hearts and Minds of our students, nurturing each student's academic, social, and emotional needs. We aim to help each student grow in her understanding and commitment to Torah and Chabad Chassidus, so she can develop a meaningful relationship with Hashem and a lasting Hiskashrus to the Rebbe.

Our goal is to inspire our students and prepare them with the knowledge and skills necessary to become Neiros Lehair, young women who impact and bring the warmth of Chassidus to their own homes, communities and friends.

## Kindling Hearts

At LGHS you will have many opportunities to strengthen your Hiskashrus, integrate one's learning b'penimius, connect to the ideas learned in 'real life', have meaningful discussions, and more. These opportunities provide the tools to fulfill the Rebbe's vision of becoming a beacon of light to the world.

### Achos HaTemimim

- A vibrant student-run program that encourages students to grow on their personal level in their Yiddishkeit (אידישקייט), Chassidishkeit (חסידישקייט), and Hiskashrus (התקשרות).

### Bnos Chabad

- Bnos Chabad is a student-run program that plans school Shabbatons, games, farbrengens and more. There are many opportunities for all students to participate and develop life skills.

### Chassidishe Yomim Tovim

- Chassidishe Yomim Tovim is a student-run program that plans special events and hachanos (הכנות) for the Chassidic special days.

### Farbrengens

- The niggunim, conversations, and stories at a farbrengen add a chayus and depth to one's studies that is a basic component to Chassidim and Chassidus. At LGHS you will have school-wide farbrengens with various speakers, and smaller class farbrengens.

### Mechaneches Program

- In the summer students in grades 10–12 pick 3 top choices of staff members they feel would be most beneficial in supporting them and their academic and ruchnius (רוחניות) growth. Students will be assigned by the beginning of the year to their point person, with effort being made to accommodate one of their choices. Anyone without choices will be assigned.
- This staff member will meet with you individually to help support your high school experience, and help you maximize your growth throughout your years at LGHS. She monitors your progress, makes herself available to hear your concerns and help you work through them.
- She is the first point of contact for students and parents.
- Shluchos (the dorm counselors) will be running class programs, farbrengens, etc.
- The Deans of Students will still oversee more global matters in their designated grades, any level changes, and in-the-moment time sensitive issues (Grades 9 & 10 → Mrs. Scharf, Grades 11 & 12 → Mrs. Perlstein).

### Mivtzoim

- Each week, and for special Chassidishe Yomim Tovim, the students arrange and go on Mivtzoim.

### Rabbi Hertz Speaks

- We are fortunate to have Rabbi Hertz address the school at special occasions, like before Yomim Tovim (ימים טובים), etc.

### Trip to the Rebbe

- The 10th grade travels as a class on a special trip to the Rebbe (נסיעה לרבי) each year.
- The 11th grade is supported and encouraged to get involved with the Kinus.
- The 12th grade has another opportunity around seminary interviewing time.

### Weekly Living Torah Video

- On Wednesdays each week the students watch the Living Torah Video of the Rebbe.

### Seder Niggunim

- On Fridays we end the day with a special Seder Niggunim.

### Pre-Yom Tov Yom Iyun

- Before Yom Tov (יום טוב) we have a Yom Iyun (יום עיון) exploring halacha (הלכה) and hashkafa (השקפה) around the Yom Tov.

### GPA System

LGHS employs a grading system based on a GPA (Grade Point Average) to allow for a student's strengths to compensate for her weaknesses. The weight of the subject in the GPA is based on the number of times a week a subject meets. Certain subjects every student also needs to pass with a 60%, as explained below.

### Report Cards

- The most effective way to earn a good grade is by fulfilling the requirements of the class through attendance, academic achievement, effort, class participation and conduct.
- Your report card grades have the following components recorded:
  - A. Subjects affecting the GPA
  - B. Citizenship/Hanhaga — affecting the GPA
  - C. Tests not affecting the GPA but required to pass with a 60% or above in order to graduate

#### A. Subjects Affecting the GPA

There are three categories of subjects affecting the GPA:

1. Academic Subjects
   - All academic subjects are averaged into the GPA. You will earn grades for Academic Subjects based on tests and major projects and reports, quizzes, homework, participation and behavior. Each subject is weighted in the GPA as many times a week as it meets.
2. Physical Education
3. Fine Arts

#### B. Citizenship/Hanhaga — affecting the GPA

- Hanhaga B'tfilla
  - Grades are based on davening attendance and respecting the davening atmosphere.
- Uniform Grade — see under Dress Code.
- Shemiras Hazman
  - Girls are graded for coming to school on time in the morning, coming to each class on time, and attending each class fully. This grade is weighted as a 5-times-a-week class in the GPA. Unexcused absences get factored into the Shemiras Hazman grade.
  - This grade must be passed every year with a 75% average to continue as an LGHS student into the next grade. It must be passed in 12th grade in order to earn a diploma. There is no make-up work.

#### C. Tests Not Affecting the GPA but Requiring a Passing Grade of 60% or Above to Graduate

1. Constitution Tests
   - You are required to pass both the State and US Constitution tests in order to receive a diploma.
   - The US Constitution test is generally administered after the study of the Constitutions in 10th grade.
   - The State Constitution test is generally administered in 12th grade.
2. Gemers — 11th and 12th Grades
   - Gemers are 4-year cumulative tests on chosen subjects, taken by eleventh and twelfth grade students. You must earn a 60% or higher on every gemer in order to graduate. Please note that if you have transferred into LGHS, you will need to make up any gemer that you missed in order to earn an LGHS diploma.
   - Gemers are administered for the following subjects:
     - 11th grade: Bekius in Chumash Inyanim; Hayom Yom; Tanya part one
     - 12th grade: Rashi (2 tests); Halacha; Tanya part two
3. CPR Training
   - It is an Illinois State requirement for every high school graduate to be certified in CPR. If a student has such a certification, she may photocopy it as proof for LGHS to place in her folder. If a student does not have such a certification, LGHS will help arrange for her to receive it before graduation.

### LGHS Diploma

#### Requirements for Earning a Diploma

- Upholding LGHS Hanhaga and standards. LGHS reserves the right to withhold a diploma if the student has shown behaviors inconsistent with the values of the LGHS community.
- Passing the following classes and requirements with at least an average of 60% by the end of each year:
  - Chumash
  - Tanya
  - Halacha
  - All gemers
  - Ivrit
  - Shemiras Hazman at 75% (no make-up work — must be passed to matriculate to the next grade)
  - English
  - Math
  - Science
  - A total of 3 years of History (and both State and Federal Constitution tests)
  - Physical Education
  - Fine Arts
  - CPR Certification
- Overall Grade Point Average (GPA):
  - For Standard Diploma — 75% GPA
  - For Honors Diploma — 90%
  - For Individualized Diploma — 75% (In certain cases, the administration of LGHS may design an individualized program to meet a student's specific academic needs. Students earn an individualized diploma when they are on an individualized program. They need to have earned a grade point average of at least 75% of her specific requirements at the time of graduation.)

#### Make-Up Work Policies for Earning a Diploma

If a student failed classes by the end of first semester:

- During the school year, she will need to work harder to bring up the average next semester.

If a student failed classes by the end of the year that are required for a diploma (9th–11th grade students):

- The student is given the opportunity to make up work over the summer for most of the subjects which need to be passed each year. There is no charge if the work is finished by September 1st of that summer.
- She may only earn between 60%–75% for that work, no more.
- There is no makeup work given for the classes that are not requirements to pass.

Graduates:

- Any student who has less than 75% GPA at the time of graduation is not eligible to receive an LGHS Diploma. There is no make-up work given to raise a GPA. She will be eligible to receive a "Certificate of Attendance" as long as her attendance record and participation in class warrant it.
- After 12th grade graduation, make-up work is only given if the GPA is 75%. The September 1st following the graduation of the student is the deadline for this work in order for the student to be eligible for an LGHS diploma. After the deadline, the charge will be $400 to receive an LGHS diploma during the first year after graduation, and $700 during the second year after graduation. After two years, she will no longer be eligible for an LGHS diploma.

#### Transfer Student Requirements

- All transfer students to LGHS must have completed the LGHS requirements in order to earn an LGHS diploma as would any other student. As a result, they may have some makeup work to do from their previous schooling. This will be determined when we receive the transcripts from the other schools she has attended. Please refer to the above requirements for all students.
- This means that if a transfer student is missing any of the above subjects from her previous schooling, she may need to make up some coursework to be eligible for a diploma.

The same way that it is necessary to be in class and participate in the learning, it is important to be punctual to class. It is a sign of respect to the teacher and the subject material and an important life skill. LGHS students are expected to be in class on time.

If a student is late to a class:

1. If she was speaking with a teacher, she must get a late note from the teacher before going to her class. She will not be marked tardy if the teacher writing the note excuses her.
2. If she was late for another reason, she must go to the office to get a late note before returning to class. She will be marked tardy, and points will be taken off her Shemiras Hazman grade.

### Dress Code

We recognize and respect that each family and student may have their own standards and areas of growth. We are also aware that there is a range of opinions among Rabbanim regarding the halachos (הלכות) of tznius (צניעות).

Please note that the guidelines we are sharing are not intended to define your family's values or halachic standards. Rather, they are designed to help foster a respectful, growth-oriented environment for all students, while also being mindful of the broader community we are a part of.

#### Outside of School Hours / Extracurricular Events

Intent: Maintaining and respecting Community Halacha standards.

Guidelines:

- Elbows must be covered at all times including when hands are raised.
- Knees must be covered at all times including when sitting down.
- Collar bone must be covered front and back, as well as on the sides.
- No slits.
- Bright red may not be worn.

#### School Events — When Uniform Isn't Required

Intent: Non-uniform dress code upholds a higher school standard and aims to create a more refined atmosphere.

Guidelines:

- All outside-of-school policies are to be upheld, in addition to:
- No jeans may be worn.
- Nails should be neat and short, no tips. If choosing to wear nail polish, colors that may be worn are soft whites and soft light pinks.

#### Dress Up Days

Intent: School dress up days are designed to bring chayus (חיות) and joy while maintaining a healthy balance of school functionality, decorum and safety.

Guidelines:

- All non-uniform school event policies apply.
- No slippers or open back shoes may be worn.
- Dress up may only be female characters.
- No degrading dress up (i.e. tattoos, street wear etc.).

#### Rosh Chodesh

Intent: On Rosh Chodesh we are trying to create an elevated atmosphere around school, celebrating a special day for women.

Guidelines:

- On the first day of Rosh Chodesh, students may dress up and wear a dressy top (no denim or sweatshirts).
- A uniform skirt should be worn.

#### Birthdays

Intent: On a girl's birthday, we are trying to acknowledge and celebrate each individual.

- On a student's birthday, she may dress up and wear a dressy top (no denim or sweatshirts).

- In addition, the student must give the school receptionist all medications they need to take during school hours. This includes antibiotics, aspirin, Advil, etc.
- Only the school receptionist has permission to dispense any medication, and needs to observe the student taking the medication.
- The school allows the self-administration and self-carry of a student with asthma upon the receipt of a signed parent permission notification and a copy of the prescription, both of which need to be updated yearly.
- The school allows the self-administration and self-carry of a student with diabetes upon the receipt of a signed parent permission notification and a copy of the prescription, both of which need to be updated yearly.
- If a student has any other medication that she needs to have with her at all times (e.g. an EpiPen), the parent must inform the school office. This is a matter of safety and the law.
- If a student requires a medical cannabis-infused product, the school allows it to be administered by either a trained school nurse, trained administrator, a trained parent or guardian, or the student herself under the direct supervision of a trained nurse or school administrator. Prior to administration, a written authorization from the parent/guardian and a copy of the student's registry identification card must be provided by the parent/guardian. The medication must be stored with the school nurse or the school administrator.

#### Safety Drill Education — Tornado

When a tornado lockdown is announced:

- Students should go down to the basement away from windows to the outdoors.
- If going to the basement is not possible, students should file into the inside hallways away from windows and doors.
- Close classroom doors.
- Face the wall.
- Cover head with hands.

#### Hard Lock Down

Occurs when a threat of violence or incident jeopardizes the safety of those in the building. Lock down can be initiated by the office or by a person who recognizes a threat by pulling/pushing a panic button. A PA message will be announced across the building. It may be initiated by someone announcing, "go into lock down." In either scenario, students should make sure to:

- Remain totally quiet.
- Cover the window of classroom doors.
- Make sure the door is clicked shut and locked — most rooms lock automatically.
- Barricade the door with whatever is close by — desks etc.
- Turn off lights or anything that makes sound that can be heard from the hallway.
- Have everyone proceed to a safe spot in the room and/or sit on the floor away from windows and doors/under desks with heads covered and anything that can be thrown in hand or near you (heavy books etc).
  - If your class is in either shul room, when the B button is pressed the magnets will lock and a strobe light will flash. Make sure the door is closed all the way; the magnet will only lock the doors if they are completely closed.
  - In the big shul, hide under the tables or evacuate through the back door (if the threat is from the lobby).
  - In the Beis Medrash, sit along the wall of the doors in a way you can't be seen from the window in the door or the outside windows, behind the bimah, or a few students can go into the mikvah room.
- If anyone is in the bathroom, go into the stall, lock the door, stand on the toilet, duck your head to be within the stall, and be silent.
- If you are in any common area, go into the nearest room.
- Do not open the door, even for a student.
- Do not let in someone who says he is a policeman or any other authority.
- Do the above and wait for further instructions.

To leave a lock down:

- You will receive a text on your phone, or
- An administrator or police officer will go around and give the release word.

#### Soft Lock Down

Occurs when something outside the building is a potential danger to those inside the building. The signal for a soft lockdown is an administrator or office personnel announcing it, on the PA system/phone paging system, or when the strobe light is flashing on the front doors and the magnet is locked.

- No one enters or exits the building.
- Within the building, normal schedule.
- Teachers shall close all shades and blinds to the outside of the building.
- Wait for an update from the administration that the lockdown is over.

#### Terrorist Confrontation

Occurs when a terrorist is in a shared open area. Each student should do the following:

- If at all possible, flee the area.
- If one is exposed and flight is not possible, attack the terrorist or throw at him whatever object is available.
- If flight is not an option, hide under or behind any object in the area.

#### Evacuation

Occurs when danger inside the building requires evacuation. Students should:

- Be silent.
- Proceed to the closest exit, single file.
- Walk south.
- If a shelter is needed, students should proceed to the student center or, if directed, west down Devon to Chesed L'Avraham, 3135 W. Devon — or as directed by police.

#### School Bus Evacuation

Events requiring school bus evacuation:

- Bus fire
- Potential of fire
- Bus breaks down in a dangerous location (such as on a railroad crossing)
- Probability of flooding or rising water

Essentials of a successful evacuation:

- Familiarity with emergency doors, window exits and entrance door
- Driver remains calm and maintains order
- Students remain calm and attentive to the driver's directions

Evacuation procedures:

- Front door evacuation — driver or helper positioned outside of door, students walk out of the bus and line up quietly at a designated area.
- Rear door evacuation — driver or helper positioned outside of door, students sit and slide to the ground.
- Split door evacuation — driver will direct rows 1–5 to exit the front door, rows 6–11 exit the rear door.
- Bus roof evacuation — driver demonstrates how to open the roof hatch to climb out of the bus.
- Window evacuation — driver demonstrates how to open front and designated windows to climb out.

In all evacuations, drivers will ensure that all students have exited the school bus. Once the school bus has been evacuated, students must be 100 feet (3 bus lengths) away from the bus. The driver and helper will join students away from the bus.

#### General Safety Guidelines for All Times

- Awareness at all times of our surroundings — both outside and within the school.
- Should you see someone or something strange outside or inside, alert the school office immediately.

- Remain calm and silent during any emergency and follow instructions.

### Professional Code of Conduct for LGHS

General Safe Conduct:

- Faculty/Staff understands and accepts that s/he is a caretaker of teenagers.
- Faculty/Staff are committed to maintaining the proper relationship with the students and not sharing personal information.
- Faculty/Staff understand that there is a clear power difference between themselves and their students (authority, experience, knowledge, different sets of rules).
- Faculty/Staff understand that inappropriate language and touching, with or without abuse, of a student can have severe emotional and psychological effects on a student that can last a lifetime.
- Faculty/Staff may, under no circumstances, hit a student.
- Faculty/Staff may not physically restrain or guide a student unless in the case of clear and present danger, and then only in the presence of another staff member if possible, and with the minimal force necessary.
- Faculty/Staff may not use abusive or derogatory language (i.e. shaming, sarcastic remarks, teasing, etc.).
- Faculty/Staff will seek the assistance of supervisory administrative staff when they encounter a particularly difficult student.
- Faculty/Staff will never forbid students to share any conversations or information with parents or administrators, nor instruct students to keep secrets from parents.
- Faculty/Staff will be mindful when communicating with students via students' personal cell phones, or via text messages, email, and will specifically consider the power differential of their relationship when doing so.
- Faculty/Staff will only take pictures of and with students when it is professionally appropriate and with the student's permission.
- Faculty/Staff may not be secluded with a student in an area that is not accessible and visible to others.
- In all dealings with students, faculty/staff strive to respond as opposed to react to students; Faculty/Staff do not respond to a student in anger.
- Faculty/Staff have no contact in a place on a student's body that is normally covered by a bathing suit (unless administering medication and then only with another adult present).
- Faculty/Staff never have contact against a child's will (unless in the case of clear and present danger).
- Faculty/Staff touch, when appropriate, is restricted to only students' hands, shoulders or the upper back (always in the presence of others).
- Faculty/Staff will not have contact when the student verbally or nonverbally signals discomfort.
- Faculty/Staff will ask for support when dealing with escalating stress or difficult situations.
- Faculty/Staff will alert senior or supervisory staff to dangerous or at-risk situations between students and staff, between students and students, or any student individually.
- Faculty/Staff are aware that they are a mandated reporter and the ramifications of this responsibility.
